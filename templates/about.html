Early Detection of Sepsis

Project Overview

Early Detection of Sepsis is a disease-specific, non-ICU clinical decision-support system developed using Machine Learning. The objective is to identify patients at risk of sepsis before ICU admission, enabling early medical intervention and improved survival outcomes.

What is Sepsis?

Sepsis is a life-threatening condition caused by the body’s extreme immune response to infection. If not detected early, it can rapidly progress to organ failure and death. Early identification significantly increases the probability of successful treatment.

Why Disease-Specific & Non-ICU?

  • Most existing models focus on ICU patient data
  • Early prediction is more valuable before ICU admission
  • Different infections lead to sepsis through different clinical pathways

Therefore, this system uses disease-specific models trained independently for:

  • Pneumonia
  • Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)
  • Intra-Abdominal Infection
  • Skin & Soft Tissue Infection (SSTI)

System Architecture

Each disease has an independently trained Machine Learning model. At runtime:

  • The selected disease determines which model, scaler, and feature configuration are loaded.
  • Input features are dynamically aligned with the trained model structure.
  • The appropriate serialized model generates the prediction.

This modular architecture ensures scalability, maintainability, and disease-wise performance optimization.

Prediction Strategy

The system follows a two-stage prediction mechanism:

  • Binary Classification: Predicts whether the patient is at risk of sepsis (Yes / No).
  • Probability Estimation: The model outputs a confidence score (0–1) representing the estimated likelihood of sepsis.
  • Risk Stratification: Based on probability thresholds:
    • Low Risk → Probability < 0.30
    • Medium Risk → 0.30 – 0.60
    • High Risk → > 0.60

Model Confidence represents the predicted probability. For example, a confidence of 0.58 indicates an estimated 58% likelihood of sepsis according to the trained classifier.

Machine Learning Model

The system uses a Random Forest Classifier due to its:

  • High accuracy on structured clinical datasets
  • Ability to model nonlinear relationships
  • Robustness against overfitting
  • Feature importance analysis capability

Clinical Features Used

  • Heart Rate
  • Respiratory Rate
  • Systolic Blood Pressure
  • Platelet Count
  • Disease-specific clinical indicators
